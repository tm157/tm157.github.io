Learning Articulated Human Textures from Single Image

Published 08 Dec 2018.

We present a methodology to infer a textured and artic-ulated 3D model of a person from a single image. We usea rich and compact mesh representation as the basis of ourarticulated human model and detail it with a predicted tex-ture. We propose a two-stream approach that decouples ge-ometry and texture inference, and combines the outputs ofthe two streams using a differentiable renderer, which en-ables end-to-end self-supervised learning. In order to pre-dict a consistent texture that captures the nuances of theinput image, we propose two crucial techniques â€“ 1) gen-eration of an incomplete UV texture map and 2) multiviewcurriculum training. We show through our experiments thatour network is able to learn appropriate priors from thedataset and predict a complete texture of the human modelthat captures the regularities in appearance such as symme-try in clothing and human body priors, thereby generatingrealistic human models. We further show that our artic-ulated human models can be animated by control policieslearned from motion clips, thereby making the person in theinput image come alive
